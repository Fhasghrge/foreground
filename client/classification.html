The Classification of Foregrounding: Deviation and Parallelism


Deviation
Refers to the choice of linguistic elements out of the regular norm. Here are eight types of deviation.
  • Grammatical Deviation: refers to the linguistic distortion of each single unit and internal structural form by means of misplacement or replacement.
  •  Semantic Deviation: occurs when a wordâ€™s meaning goes against the expectation formed by the surrounding words.
  • Lexical Deviation: occurs when a new word is created or a common word has new meanings. 
  • Phonological Deviation: occurs in some phonological rhetoric, such as alliteration. 
  • Graphological Deviation: occurs in the case when the writing of words are against the norms of writing. Semantic deviation signifies illogical linguistic relations. 
  • Deviation of Register: occurs when one context of particular register borrows vocabularies of another. 
  • Dialectic Deviation:  occurs when the author borrows language of another community to express his thoughts. 
  • Deviation of Historic Periods: implies the usage of language bearing different meaning for the on-going of times, such as 14 the usage of Latinate language.


Parallelism
The repetition of words or sentences that sharing similar ideas and grammatical patterns. Here are two types of parallelism.
  • Parallel structure: refers to the structure formed by laying together two or more words, phrases, clauses or sentences that share identical or similar grammatical organization. 

  • Repetition: means words or sentences repeatedly used in literary works to convey an emphatic effect. It is another kind of quantitative foregrounding widely used in the source text.
